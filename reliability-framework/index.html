RIF-02 / v2.0

Operating Strategy for a Reliability Org

North Star

Enable sustained engineering velocity through immediate and long term reliability initiatives that span infrastructure, developer tooling & enablement, process, partnerships across domains, education and automation.

Operating Values
Prioritization operates within these constraints
The values are the guardrails. They prevent prioritization from going off the rails.
Every initiative traces to a business outcome
If you can't draw the line, question the work.
Know the who
Your work draws a causal line to the end user, no matter how deep in the stack.
Collective action
We choose actions for collective success and build the organizational observability to recognize and reward this.
Shared ownership
Application and reliability teams are jointly accountable for and rewarded for org-level reliability outcomes.
We own the practices and frameworks that make reliability systematic.

Prioritization Engine

How we decide what to work on
1. Rank by business criticality
Leadership ranks business outcomes:
existential, critical, important
2. Evaluate within tiers
Assess impact:effort ratio. Weight foundational work that unblocks other initiatives.
3. Account for time sensitivity
Hard deadlines override pure impact:effort.
4. Manage uncertainty deliberately
YOLO is still YOLO, even on reliability. Timebox a spike before committing full resources.
Operational Mechanisms
SLOs
Linked to business outcomes. The contract between reliability and the business.
Capacity Planning
Without it, strategy is aspiration. Ensures initiatives are resourced to succeed.
Strategic Roadmap
Living artifact. Output of the prioritization engine, reviewed and evolved continuously.
Cross-functional Planning
Reliability outcomes require partnership. Planning happens with product, infra, and security.

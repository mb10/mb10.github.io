Inside the Lives of Asylum Seekers in Germany

For Family & Opportunity

FARZAM and AHMAD, 17 and 16, are both from Afghanistan and they have been in Germany since December 2015.


Click to see 360 video

Ahmad and Farzam are both from Afghanistan. They traveled separately to German and both arrived in the Austrian border town of Passau in December, 2015. They stay at a refugees center for boys for about two to three weeks before being placed elsewhere.

Matthias Schacherbauer who works for the youth refugee center in Passau, says the number of young people (under 18) arriving to Germany has increased substantially in the last year. From 2014 to 2015 the number of youth asylum seekers increased by 300 percent. As of November 2015, an estimated 30 percent of those applying for asylum were under 18.

As Scherbacher says, the German word is anchorperson. The boys are meant to come establish themselves and obtain papers so their families may follow. Farzam and Ahmad are both eager to study and improve their German.

Ahmad is now in Gummersbach, in western Germany and Farzam is now in Thueringen, in central Germany.
